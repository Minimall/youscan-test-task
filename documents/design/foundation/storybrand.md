# StoryBrand: YouScan alerting

## Character (the hero)

A B2B operator inside a brand, agency, or comms team — most often a PR specialist, SMM, or Brand Manager — who is responsible for catching the things that matter in their brand's online conversation before someone above them asks why they didn't.

---

## Problem

**External:** Their topic collects thousands of mentions a day. They cannot read all of them, and the ones that matter are buried.

**Internal:** They don't trust the alerts they already get. Most fire for nothing; the one time something real happened, the alert looked the same as the other forty that week. They feel either anxious ("am I missing something?") or numb ("I've stopped opening these").

**Philosophical:** Their job is to catch signal. A tool that buries them in noise isn't helping them do their job — it's making them worse at it.

---

## Guide

YouScan, with a credible track record in social listening and an ML stack that already classifies sentiment, authors, and topics. The guide's authority comes from the existing platform. What's new is empathy for the alert-fatigue problem specifically.

---

## Plan

1. Pick a preset that matches your role, or start from scratch.
2. See exactly what the rule would have done in the last 30 days before you save it.
3. When an alert fires, decide and act in one screen — and edit the rule from that same screen if it was wrong.

---

## Call to action

**Direct:** "Create your first alert."

**Transitional:** "See how a rule would behave on your topic — no commitment."

---

## Stakes

**Failure (without us):** You miss a real crisis because it looked like the forty fake ones, or you spend an hour a day clearing alerts you didn't need.

**Success (with us):** You get fewer alerts, you trust the ones you get, and when one is wrong you fix the rule in two clicks instead of muting and forgetting.

---

## Identity transformation

From "the person drowning in notifications" to "the person whose alerts are the early-warning system the rest of the team relies on."
